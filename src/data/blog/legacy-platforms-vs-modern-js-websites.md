## Legacy platforms 

WordPress, Shopify type of platforms combine content management, theming, and hosting into a single, easy‑to‑use system. They excel at time‑to‑market: you can launch a fully functional site in days with minimal technical skills. 

Their vast plugin ecosystems and visual editors make them ideal for non‑technical teams, standard content sites, and straightforward e‑commerce stores. However, they can suffer from performance bottlenecks, security vulnerabilities from third‑party add‑ons, and maintenance overhead as updates and plugins accumulate.

---

## Modern JS frameworks

React, Next.js, Vue Js, Nuxt, SvelteKit with a headless CMS separate the frontend from the backend, delivering exceptional performance through static generation and server‑side rendering. 

They offer component‑based architectures that simplify maintenance, reduce security exposure (no direct database on the frontend), and enable parallel workflows—developers work in Git while content editors use a purpose‑built CMS. The trade‑off is a higher initial development cost and a steeper learning curve for non‑developers.

---

## Choosing between them

It depends on your team, budget, and goals. If you need speed, low upfront cost, and a single interface for a small team, legacy platforms are a reliable choice. 

If performance, security, and scalable team collaboration are critical—and you have developer resources—modern frameworks provide a future‑proof foundation.

---

## Conclusion  
For rapid, low‑complexity projects, start with a traditional platform. For ambitious digital experiences that demand agility and scalability, invest in a modern framework.
